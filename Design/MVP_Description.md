# VocaES — Feature Overview

A Spanish vocabulary trainer that connects what you've learned to real-world content, so you can see exactly how much you already understand.

---

## The Idea

Most vocabulary apps teach words in isolation. VocaES ties your learning to real Spanish content — Netflix shows, podcasts, and news articles — and shows you, word by word, how much of it you can already read. The further you progress, the more content unlocks.

---

## Vocabulary Cards

At the heart of the app is a simple card mechanic. You see a Spanish word, tap it, and it flips to reveal the English translation plus a real example sentence. Learning feels tactile and low-pressure — no tests, no timers, just you and the word.

---

## Dashboard & Progress

The main screen is built around a single progress bar showing how many of the 1,500 words you've learned. Upcoming milestones are always visible, so you can see exactly what you're working toward next. It's motivating without being pushy.

---

## Milestones

Every few hundred words, you hit a milestone that unlocks something new:

- **100 words** — first podcast snippet, first glimpse at your comprehension score
- **250 words** — first Netflix transcript unlocks
- **500 words** — first news article, full comprehension breakdown
- **750 words** — access to the full rotating content library
- **1,000 words** — ability to paste your own Spanish text and see your score
- **1,250 words** — full Netflix episode transcripts, personal stats over time
- **1,500 words** — completion, word gap analysis, certificate

---

## Content Insight

This is the signature feature. Open any unlocked piece of content — a Netflix episode, a podcast transcript, a news article — and every word is color-coded based on whether you've learned it:

- **Green** — you know this word
- **Yellow** — you've seen it but aren't sure
- **Red** — not yet learned

At the top you get a single number: the percentage of this content you should already understand. That number updates automatically as you learn more words, even for content you've looked at before.

---

## Content Sources

- 🎬 **Netflix** — subtitles and transcripts from Spanish-language shows
- 🎙️ **Podcasts** — episodes from Spanish-learning and native-speaker podcasts
- 📰 **Articles** — pieces from Spanish publications, ranging from simplified to native-level
