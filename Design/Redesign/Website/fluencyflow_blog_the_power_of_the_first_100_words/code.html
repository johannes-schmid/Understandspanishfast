Learning a language often feels like climbing an infinite mountain. Traditional methods throw thousands of words at you from day one—colors, animals, household objects, and complex grammatical structures that you might not use for years.

The 80/20 Rule of Linguistics

Language follows the Pareto Principle: 80% of your results come from 20% of your efforts. In Spanish, this is even more extreme. Studies of linguistic corpora show that the top 100 most frequent words account for roughly 50% of all spoken language.

Why 100?

"By mastering the first 100, you aren't just learning words; you're building the scaffolding that allows you to understand the context of the other 50% you don't know yet."

What's in the First 100?

It’s not just about nouns like "cat" or "house." The true power players are the words that glue ideas together:

  • bolt Structural Verbs: Essential for stating existence, movement, and desire (e.g., hay, ser, estar, ir, querer).
  • link Connectors: Words that turn fragments into sentences (e.g., y, o, pero, porque).
  • location_on Prepositions: Providing spatial and temporal context (e.g., en, de, a, con).
