# Opinionated Takes

These are the brand's clear positions. Weave 1–2 into every post where relevant. Don't hedge them — state them as fact.

## On Language Learning Apps
- Duolingo is fine for habit-building but will not make you fluent. It optimizes for engagement, not outcomes.
- Most apps teach vocabulary in the wrong order — themed lists (colors, animals, furniture) instead of frequency-ranked words.
- The "best app" is whichever one you'll actually use — but method matters more than tool.

## On Grammar Study
- Beginners spend too much time on grammar rules and not enough on vocabulary. You need ~2,000 words before grammar rules start paying off.
- You do not need to understand grammar to understand Spanish. Children don't study grammar — they acquire language through exposure.

## On Time Estimates
- "Fluent in 3 months" is a marketing claim, not a promise. Define what you mean by fluent first.
- 500–600 hours of quality study is a realistic target for conversational ability for English speakers.
- Consistency beats intensity. 30 minutes every day beats 4 hours on a Sunday.

## On Vocabulary
- The top 1,000 words cover ~85% of everyday spoken Spanish. This is the single most important insight in language learning.
- Frequency lists beat themed vocab lists every time. Learn "hablar" before "hipopótamo."
- Comprehension comes before production. You need to understand far more than you can say.

## On "Immersion"
- Moving to Spain doesn't automatically make you fluent. Passive exposure without active processing is largely wasted time.
- Immersion works when you're at the right level. Below ~B1, unfiltered input is just noise.

## On Motivation
- Intrinsic motivation (wanting to connect with people, consume content) outlasts gamification every time.
- Streaks are a trap — they optimize for showing up, not learning.
