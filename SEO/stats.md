# Key Stats & Data Points

Use these to add credibility and specificity. Always cite the source inline.

## Vocabulary & Comprehension
- The top 1,000 most frequent Spanish words cover ~85% of everyday spoken Spanish (Nation, 2001 — vocabulary research)
- The top 2,000 words cover ~90%+ of spoken language
- Native speakers use ~5,000–7,000 word families in daily conversation
- Knowing 95% of words on a page is the threshold for comfortable reading without a dictionary (Nation)

## Time to Learn Spanish (for English speakers)
- The FSI (Foreign Service Institute) classifies Spanish as a Category I language — approximately 600–750 classroom hours for professional working proficiency
- That's roughly 24–30 weeks of full-time study, or ~2 years at 1 hour/day
- Spanish is one of the easiest languages for English speakers — compared to 2,200 hours for Japanese or Mandarin

## Spanish Language Scale
- ~500 million native Spanish speakers worldwide (Instituto Cervantes)
- Spanish is the 2nd most spoken language by native speakers globally
- Over 41 million Spanish speakers in the US alone (US Census Bureau)
- Spanish is spoken in 20+ countries

## Learning App Stats
- Duolingo has 500M+ registered users but ~96% drop out within the first month (various estimates)
- Babbel reports users achieve basic conversational ability in 3 months with 15 min/day (their own study — treat with skepticism)

## Comprehension & Input
- Stephen Krashen's Input Hypothesis: language is acquired through comprehensible input (i+1 — input slightly above current level)
- Comprehensible input studies suggest you need ~10,000 hours of exposure for native-level fluency

## Reading Level
- A1 learner: ~500 words; A2: ~1,000; B1: ~2,000; B2: ~4,000; C1: ~8,000; C2: ~16,000+ (CEFR estimates)
