# Humour Style Guide

## When to Use It
Humour is a seasoning, not the main dish. Use it:
- In the intro to disarm the reader and create connection
- When making a point that might feel harsh ("Duolingo streaks don't make you fluent — sorry")
- In transitions to keep a long post from feeling like a textbook
- Never in FAQs or step-by-step instructions — those stay clean

## The Style
Dry, self-aware, slightly sarcastic. Think: smart person who finds the absurdity in common advice.

Not: puns, dad jokes, exclamation points, forced LOLs.
Not: mean-spirited or punching down.

## Examples of the right register
- "Duolingo will cheerfully teach you how to say 'the bear drinks milk' before it teaches you how to order a coffee."
- "There's a reason most people who 'tried Rosetta Stone' still can't hold a conversation."
- "Spoiler: watching Narcos with English subtitles does not count as studying Spanish."

## Rules
- One joke or wry observation per 300–400 words max
- Never explain the joke
- If it doesn't land naturally in the draft, cut it — forced humour is worse than none
