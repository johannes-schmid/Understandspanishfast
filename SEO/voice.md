# Brand Voice Guide

## Core Tone
Direct, confident, outcome-focused. Write like a smart friend who has done the research and is giving you the straight answer — not a textbook, not a salesman.

## Reading Level
Aim for Grade 8–10. Short sentences. No jargon unless explained immediately. Favor active voice.

## Sentence Structure
- Vary length: short punchy sentences after longer explanatory ones
- Lead with the point, then support it
- Never bury the lede — answer the question in the first 2–3 sentences

## What to Avoid
- "Fun and engaging" — never say this
- Passive voice ("it is said that…") — just say it
- Hedging ("it might be", "some people feel") — be decisive
- Filler intro paragraphs ("In today's fast-paced world…") — cut them
- Generic edtech buzzwords: interactive, immersive, gamified, engaging

## What to Use
- Specific numbers and timeframes ("80% of everyday Spanish", "1,500 words", "3 months")
- Second person ("you") — talk directly to the reader
- Strong verbs — avoid "is" and "are" where a better verb exists
- Contractions — they're fine, they sound human

## Structure
- Use H2s for main sections, H3s sparingly for sub-points
- Bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Bold key takeaways within paragraphs (sparingly — only the most important line)
- Keep paragraphs to 2–4 sentences max

## Intro Formula
1. State the reader's situation or frustration (1 sentence)
2. Answer the main question directly (1–2 sentences)
3. Tell them what this post covers (1 sentence)
