Frequently asked questions

How many Spanish words do I need to understand everyday conversations? expand_more
Research shows that the 1,000 most common words give you roughly 74% coverage of everyday spoken Spanish. To reach 80%, you need around 2,000 words. For near-native comprehension, 5,000–8,000 words is the long-term target. The critical insight: learning by frequency gives you the fastest path — each word you learn from this list has more impact than words from any other source.
What percentage of Spanish can I understand with 1,000 words? expand_more
With the 1,000 most common Spanish words, you can understand approximately 74% of everyday spoken Spanish. That means roughly 3 out of every 4 words in a typical conversation will be familiar to you. Coverage is higher in informal speech and lower in technical writing. With 2,000 words you reach ~80%, and the returns continue to improve — but at a slower rate.
Are these words ranked by frequency? expand_more
Yes. This list is ordered by corpus frequency — how often each word actually appears in real spoken and written Spanish. The ordering draws on frequency research including Mark Davies' analysis of hundreds of millions of words of Spanish text. Word #1 (de) appears dramatically more often than word #1,000. The top 10 words alone account for roughly 25% of all words you'll encounter.
What is the fastest way to learn these words? expand_more
The most efficient method is spaced repetition — reviewing words just before you're about to forget them. Combined with learning in frequency order (highest impact words first), you can reach functional comprehension faster than any other approach.
